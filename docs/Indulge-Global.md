## 2. The Problem They Solve

Indulge Global exists for a simple but underserved reality: **the ultra-wealthy are rich in resources but poor in time.**

- High-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth individuals (HNIs/UHNIs) have wealth, but **managing access and the operations around it is a hassle** — last-minute reservations, sold-out event tickets, rare product sourcing, travel logistics across time zones.
- Personal assistants are limited: they handle local errands but can't get **Wimbledon tickets on 48 hours' notice**, don't work weekends, and lack global brand relationships. Indulge positions itself as the global, always-on alternative — 24/7/365.
- Post-COVID, founder Karan Bhangay noticed a behavioural shift: affluent consumers stopped wanting phone calls, apps, and long processes. They wanted **speed, familiarity, and simplicity** — which is why the service runs primarily on WhatsApp. One message, infinite reach.
- Privacy and discretion are core requirements for this clientele, baked into the service model.

**The origin was organic:** clients of Bhangay's earlier luxury businesses began messaging him on WhatsApp for help with reservations, travel, and sourcing. The volume of inbound requests revealed a structural gap, and a dedicated product was built around it.

## 3. Vision & Positioning

- **Vision statement (Advita Bihani):** "Bring the world to our patrons' fingertips" — to be the go-to problem solvers clients trust with a simple "just tell them, they'll figure it out."
- **Philosophy:** Modern luxury is not about owning things — it's about **emotions, connections, experiences, and memories**. On the Raj Shamani podcast (Dec 2025), Bhangay argued that luxury in India is increasingly defined by experiences over ownership, with concierge work focused on arranging private, niche, ultra-luxury moments that were once inaccessible.
- **Three pillars:** personalization, convenience, and technology — "AI meets human touch."
- **Stated mission (Bhangay):** reshape the lives of Indulge's managers and clients alike, catalysing efficient and transformative living. "Personalization is the new currency of luxury."
- **Growth ambition:** an earlier stated goal of serving 12,000 families worldwide by 2025; post-funding goal of onboarding 1,000+ paying members. By 2026, positioning had matured into a "private command centre" model — tracking member preferences, anticipating needs across time zones, and acting before being asked.
- **Differentiators repeatedly claimed in press:** transparent pricing (a 0% commission model highlighted on Shark Tank; elsewhere a 5% brand-partner commission was cited in the pitch), referral-driven community growth, human Genies over pure automation, and absolute discretion.

## 4. Founders

### Karan Bhangay — Founder & CEO

- Middle-class upbringing; father was a state-level cricket player, mother a painter/handicrafts enthusiast; schooled at DAV. Press variously roots him in Hyderabad.
- Enrolled in engineering at **Jawaharlal Nehru Engineering College** but dropped out in his fourth year (2008), convinced it wasn't his path.
- **Entrepreneurial journey before Indulge:**
  - Launched a **luxury products magazine** (Avantgarde) — successful on ad revenue, later shut down as digital media rose.
  - Built **The Indian Luxury Expo (TILE)** — luxury exhibitions/expos for HNIs, growing to a ~40-person team. Spent 15+ years overall in the events and luxury industry.
  - Two other ventures: **Hand Painted Stories** (gifting) and **The Earth Love** (organic products marketplace).
- During COVID, past clients began requesting bespoke services over WhatsApp — sparking Indulge.
- Personal: trained squash player, avid traveller, has a pet named Gulzar.
- Advice he gives founders: transparency — in your own thoughts, your teams, products, and customers.

### Advita Bihani — Co-Founder & COO ("Master Genie 🧞‍♀️")

- Entered the workforce straight after 12th grade, taking varied gigs in Singapore, Ukraine, Australia and elsewhere; holds a BBA from Whistling Woods International.
- Early career: Marketing & Kids Content Writer at Volga Infotech; then Management Trainee and Marketing Manager at Benchmark Six Sigma, where she learned process discipline.
- Met Bhangay via LinkedIn; chose Indulge deliberately because she didn't want a mass-market business — she wanted to build something meaningful. Joined hands with him at founding.
- Featured in **YourStory HerStory's "100 Emerging Women Leaders" (2024)**. Says she's never faced gender bias as a founder; her operating mantra: "You pick it, you fix it."
- Focus area: structuring an inherently unorganised business, team capability, and operations across 180+ countries.

## 6. Services & Product

### Core service pillars (per indulge.global)

1. **Concierge** — a personal lifestyle team available around the clock: last-minute reservations to once-in-a-lifetime experiences; discreet, detail-driven, anticipatory.
2. **Networking** — exclusive events and curated retreats connecting leaders, investors, creators, and changemakers.
3. **Shop** — an exclusive marketplace for fashion, art, watches, collectibles, and rare lifestyle objects, personally sourced and authenticated ("Desire anything. We find it.").
4. **Global Events** — VIP access to Fashion Weeks, Grand Prix, Wimbledon, Cannes, Art Basel, sold-out concerts, sporting finals.

### Full offering (App Store / Play Store listing)

- Bespoke global travel (tailored itineraries, luxury hotels, private jets)
- Fine dining access (priority bookings, private dining, exclusive soirées)
- Luxury personal shopping (rare finds, limited drops, insider access)
- Curated experiences (island festivals to red-carpet premieres)
- Global sports & music events (VIP arena/stage access)
- Wellness, gifting & private event planning
- Elite retail access (designer ateliers, custom fittings)
- Power networking (private clubs, boardroom intros, curated circles)
- Emergency support (air ambulance arrangements have featured in both marketing and real client stories — e.g., a night-time medical emergency handled for a client in Bali)

### How it works

- **WhatsApp-first**: each member gets a private WhatsApp group with a team of ~6–7 globally experienced concierge professionals — the **"Genies"** — available 24/7/365. The app is a complementary channel.
- **AI + human**: an AI layer attuned to member behaviour, interests, and preferences supports human concierge managers who add judgment and the personal touch.

## 7. Shark Tank India — Season 4, Episode 1 (aired January 2025)

- **The ask:** ₹50 lakh for 1% equity → **₹50 Cr valuation**.
- **Sharks on the panel:** Vineeta Singh, Namita Thapar, Aman Gupta, Anupam Mittal, Peyush Bansal.
- **Numbers shared:** 183 paying clients; membership at ₹40K/month or ₹4L/year plus expenses; 5% commission from brand partners; ~₹2.1 Cr revenue cited in coverage.
- **Memorable moment:** the story of delivering a can of **Diet Coke to a remote Himalayan village for ₹4,000** captivated the audience and became the episode's most-quoted anecdote.
- **The room's reaction:** Peeyush joked that Namita arrives on set with her own concierge; Aman Gupta couldn't relate, arguing wealthy people already have executive assistants; the panel broadly worried about **scalability** of a high-touch, low-volume model.
- **Outcome: no deal** — but valuable mentorship and a massive visibility spike. Phones "jammed with requests" after airing, per the founders.
- **Founder takeaways:** Bhangay called opening Season 4 "a pivotal moment"; Bihani called it humbling and exhilarating, and credited the panel's feedback for refining their India/global strategy — directly leading to the cheaper **Indulge Blue** tier to widen the funnel.
- Bhangay later said Shark Tank was a game-changer for visibility and validation, and helped connect them with the right investors — accelerating the $1M raise that closed weeks later.

## 14. Market & Competition

- **Market size context:** the concierge services market was estimated at ~$751M (2021), projected to ~$1.2B by 2030 (≈6.1% CAGR), with Indulge expecting double-digit growth in India.
- **India's wealth wave:** ~13,263 UHNIs in 2023, projected to reach ~19,908 by 2028 (a ~50% jump); other estimates cite ~14K UHNIs growing to 20K in 2025 and 42K by 2027.
- **Named competitors:** Quintessentially Lifestyle, RedBeryl, A La Concierge, AICE, Pinch — plus Swiggy's "Rare Club" pilot (₹50K/yr) signalling mainstream platforms eyeing the space. Tracxn counts 13 active competitors, 2 of them funded.
- **Indulge's moat claims:** referral-only community trust, global brand relationships, WhatsApp-native speed, 24/7 human Genies, and discretion.
